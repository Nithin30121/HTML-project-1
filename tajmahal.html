Taj Mahal – Jewel of Northern India

Introduction

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is one of the most iconic monuments in the world. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it stands as a symbol of eternal love and Mughal architectural mastery.

Made entirely of white marble, it attracts millions of visitors every year and is considered one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Architecture & Design

The Taj Mahal represents the finest Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Indian, and Islamic styles. Its symmetrical layout, intricate carvings, inlay work, and calligraphy make it a masterpiece.

The central dome rises 240 feet high and is surrounded by four minarets, making the structure visually stunning from every angle.

History & Legacy

Construction began in 1632 and took nearly 22 years, involving thousands of artisans. Today, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a global symbol of beauty.
