Philosophical heritage

Introduction

India’s sacred literature is one of the oldest and most profound knowledge systems in the world. It includes spiritual, philosophical, poetic, and narrative traditions preserved for thousands of years.

The Vedas, Upanishads, epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata, and devotional texts shaped the cultural and spiritual foundation of the nation.

The Vedas – Foundations of Knowledge

The Vedas are the oldest and most authoritative scriptures of Hindu tradition. Composed in Sanskrit, they contain hymns, rituals, philosophy, and cosmic principles.

  • Rig Veda – hymns and cosmic knowledge
  • Sama Veda – melodies & chants
  • Yajur Veda – rituals & ceremonies
  • Atharva Veda – everyday wisdom, healing

The Vedas are considered the source of spiritual and scientific thought in ancient India.

Epic Literature – Ramayana & Mahabharata

India’s two great epics are more than stories—they are moral, political, and philosophical guides.

  • Ramayana – story of Lord Rama, symbol of dharma and virtue
  • Mahabharata – world’s longest epic containing the Bhagavad Gita

These timeless narratives continue to shape Indian values, culture, and beliefs across generations.

Other Sacred Texts

  • Upanishads – spiritual dialogues on self & universe
  • Puranas – myths, legends & cosmic cycles
  • Bhakti Literature – devotional hymns of saints

Together, these scriptures represent the spiritual heart of India’s civilization.
