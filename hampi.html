Hampi – Glory of the Vijayanagara Empire

Introduction to Hampi

Hampi, located in Karnataka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the ancient capital of the powerful Vijayanagara Empire. The city flourished in the 14th–16th centuries and was one of the richest and largest cities in the world.

Today, its ruins include temples, palaces, markets, and massive stone structures that tell the story of its majestic past.

Architecture & Design

The architecture of Hampi blends Dravidian style with intricate carvings. Iconic monuments include the Virupaksha Temple, Vittala Temple with its musical pillars, and the famous Stone Chariot.

Granite rock formations, massive boulders, and stunning temple complexes make Hampi one of the most unique landscapes in India.

Historical Significance

Hampi was once the cultural and economic heart of South India. It attracted traders from Persia, Portugal, and the Middle East.

After the Battle of Talikota in 1565, the city was destroyed, but its ruins remain a magnificent reminder of its glorious past.
