Rani Ki Vav – Queen’s Stepwell of India

Introduction to Rani Ki Vav

Rani Ki Vav, located in Patan, Gujarat, is an intricately constructed stepwell built in the 11th century by Queen Udayamati in memory of King Bhima I.

This UNESCO World Heritage Site is known for its architectural brilliance and mesmerizing stone carvings depicting gods, goddesses, and mythological themes.

Architectural Grandeur

Designed in the Maru-Gurjara architectural style, the stepwell consists of seven levels of deep terraces with over 1500 detailed sculptures.

The precision, symmetry, and artistry make it one of the finest stepwells ever built.

Symbol of Water Culture in India

Stepwells like Rani Ki Vav were vital for water conservation in ancient India. This magnificent structure symbolizes the importance of water in a semi-arid region.

Its restoration brought global recognition, and it stands as a jewel of Indian heritage.
