Qutub Minar – Ancient Victory Tower

Introduction

Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, is a 73-meter tall UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, it marks the beginning of Muslim rule in India.

The minar is known for its stunning Indo-Islamic architecture and remains one of India’s most visited landmarks.

Architecture

Qutub Minar is constructed using red sandstone and marble. It consists of five tapering storeys, with balconies featuring intricate carvings.

Its walls contain detailed Arabic and Brahmi inscriptions, making it an important cultural record.

Historical Importance

The tower stands within the Qutub complex, which also includes the Iron Pillar, known for its rust-resistant metallurgy.

Qutub Minar symbolizes the rise of the Delhi Sultanate and remains a testament to India’s medieval architectural brilliance.
