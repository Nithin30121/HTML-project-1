Medicine & Surgery (Ayurveda)

Foundations of Ayurveda

Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of medicine, blends herbal knowledge, holistic practices, and surgical techniques described in texts like Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita.

Emphasizing balance of body, mind, and environment, Ayurveda developed detailed diagnostics and treatments.

Classical Texts & Surgical Innovations

Sushruta’s surgical compendium describes early cataract surgery, plastic reconstruction techniques, and a comprehensive list of surgical instruments. Charaka’s medical treatises outline diagnostic methods, herbal pharmacopeia, and lifestyle medicine.

These texts reflect a sophisticated clinical tradition with empirical observation and systematic practice.

Herbal Science, Diet & Preventive Care

Ayurveda emphasizes diet (ahara), daily routine (dinacharya), and seasonal practices (ritucharya). An extensive herbal pharmacopeia and personalized treatment plans aimed at restoring dosha balance.

Many Ayurvedic concepts have modern parallels in preventive and integrative medicine.

Key Elements

  • Charaka Samhita & Sushruta Samhita
  • Surgical techniques & instruments
  • Herbal formulations & rasayanas
  • Preventive lifestyle medicine
