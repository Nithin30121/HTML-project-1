Charminar – The Icon of Hyderabad

Introduction

Charminar, built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, is the iconic symbol of Hyderabad. It was constructed to mark the end of a devastating plague and stands as a testament to Indo-Islamic architecture.

The monument features four grand minarets and overlooks the vibrant Laad Bazaar.

Architectural Brilliance

Charminar is built in granite and lime mortar with exquisite arches on all four sides. Each minaret rises 56 meters high and contains spiral staircases inside.

The structure blends Persian, Indian, and Islamic architectural elements.

Cultural Importance

Charminar is not just a monument — it is the heart of old Hyderabad. It stands beside the historic Mecca Masjid and the bustling markets famous for pearls, bangles, and spices.

The site reflects the soul of the Deccan region and its cultural fusion.
