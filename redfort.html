Red Fort – Pride of Delhi

Introduction

The Red Fort, located in the heart of Delhi, is one of India's most historically significant monuments. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648, it served as the main residence of Mughal rulers for nearly 200 years.

Constructed of red sandstone, the fort is known for its huge walls, exquisite palaces, and intricate carvings, showcasing Mughal grandeur.

Architecture & Structure

The Red Fort blends Persian, Timurid, and Indian architectural styles. With its massive ramparts stretching over 2 km, it includes iconic structures such as the Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, Rang Mahal, and the Pearl Mosque.

The fort’s carvings, marble inlays, and floral patterns represent the artistic excellence of the Mughal era.

Historical Importance

On 15 August 1947, Jawaharlal Nehru raised the Indian national flag from the Red Fort’s Lahori Gate — a tradition that continues every Independence Day.

Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a powerful symbol of India's freedom.
