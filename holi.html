Holi — Festival of Colors

What is Holi?

Holi celebrates the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. It is famous for joyful color play, music, dance, and community feasts.

The festival breaks social boundaries as people smear colors, forgive past grievances, and celebrate together.

Rituals & Color Play

Holi’s festivities begin with Holika Dahan (a bonfire) symbolizing the victory of good. The next day is Rangwali Holi: people throw dry colors and colored water, dance to music, and share sweets.

Regional foods like gujiya and thandai are a festive highlight.

Social & Cultural Meaning

Holi fosters social harmony, playfulness, and renewal. It erases social boundaries temporarily and reaffirms community bonds through shared celebration.

From villages to cities, Holi is a sensory celebration of color, music, and togetherness.

Quick Facts

  • Celebrated in late February or March (lunar calendar).
  • Includes Holika Dahan (bonfire) and Rangwali Holi (color day).
  • Promotes social unity and merriment.
