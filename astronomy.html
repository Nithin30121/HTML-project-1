Astronomy & Cosmology

Ancient Skywatchers

Indian astronomers developed precise observational methods, predicted eclipses, and created models of planetary motion. Works by Aryabhata, Varahamihira, and Brahmagupta advanced celestial calculation.

Observatories like Jantar Mantar and rich textual traditions show a rigorous scientific approach to the heavens.

Celestial Calculations & Observatories

Scholars developed accurate algorithms for planetary positions, eclipses, and timekeeping. Instruments and large-scale observatories allowed precise measurements that guided calendars and rituals.

This tradition blended mathematics and observational science to build reliable astronomical models.

Cosmology & Time Cycles

Indian cosmology describes vast cyclic time scales (yugas, kalpas) and philosophical views about the universe's origin, structure, and dissolution. These models influenced cultural calendars and philosophical reflection.

Observational astronomy and cosmological thought were often complementary, integrating mythic and empirical perspectives.
