PHILOSOPHY AND CULTURE

The six philosophies of India, also known as the Shatdarshanas, are the orthodox schools of Hindu thought that accept the authority of the Vedas

FESTIVALS

India celebrates a wide variety of festivals throughout the year, with some of the most prominent being Diwali, Holi, Navratri, Durga Puja, and Ganesh Chaturthi

LANGUAGES

According to the Census of India of 2001, India has 122 major languages and 1599 other languages.



HERITAGE

SCIENTIFIC HERITAGE

Indian scientific heritage includes significant contributions in mathematics, astronomy, and medicine, with early concepts of zero and decimal systems.

LINNGUISTIC HERITAGE

India's linguistic heritage is a complex and diverse tapestry, characterized by numerous languages, dialects, and a rich history of linguistic evolution

HISTORIC PLACES

WESTREN INDIA

Western India is home to magnificent historic sites like the Ajanta and Ellora Caves, showcasing ancient rock-cut architecture and Buddhist art.

NORTHREN INDIA

Northern India boasts iconic monuments like the Taj Mahal and Red Fort, symbolizing Mughal grandeur and architectural brilliance.

SOUTHEREN INDIA

Southern India is rich with heritage sites like Hampi and the Meenakshi Temple, showcasing Dravidian architecture and ancient empire glory.
