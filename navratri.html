Navratri — Festival of the Divine Feminine

About Navratri

Navratri is a nine-night festival honoring Goddess Durga and the feminine power (Shakti). It blends devotion, music, fasting, and dance traditions like Garba and Dandiya.

Each day celebrates a different form of the goddess, symbolizing strength, protection, and spiritual renewal.

Rituals & Cultural Practices

Devotees observe fasting, attend daily prayers, and participate in community dances. Temples and public spaces host celebrations with music, lights, and cultural performances.

Navratri culminates in Vijayadashami (Dussehra), symbolizing the victory of good over evil.

Dance, Devotion & Social Unity

Garba and Dandiya Raas are energetic community dances that bring people together. These interactive traditions reinforce social bonds and cultural continuity.

Navratri is a joyful expression of faith, art, and communal participation.

Fast Facts

  • Observed for nine nights, typically in September–October (lunar calendar).
  • Combines devotion (puja), fasting, dance (Garba/Dandiya), and public celebration.
  • Concludes with Vijayadashami / Dussehra in many regions.
