Mathematics & Number System

Overview

India’s contributions to mathematics are foundational — the invention of zero, the decimal system, early advances in algebra and trigonometry, and influential works by Aryabhata, Brahmagupta, and Bhaskara.

This page explores how ancient scholars formalized numerical notation, solved complex equations, and influenced math globally.

The Invention of Zero & Decimal System

The concept of zero (śūnya) and place-value decimal notation transformed computation. Indian mathematicians developed a concise symbol system that allowed efficient arithmetic and later enabled algebraic progress.

Texts and inscriptions show early use of zero and systematic numeral representation that spread to the Middle East and Europe.

Algebra, Trigonometry & Key Scholars

Aryabhata introduced trigonometric tables, while Brahmagupta and Bhaskara solved quadratic equations and discussed negative numbers and rules for arithmetic operations. Their algorithms were precise and often more advanced than contemporary approaches elsewhere.

These works laid the groundwork for later mathematical advances worldwide.
